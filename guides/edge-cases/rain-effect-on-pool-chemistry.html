Rain’s Effect on Pool Chemistry

Yes—rain dilutes sanitizer and salt, can lower alkalinity slightly in soft water, drags in debris and nitrogen sources, and may shift pH depending on local rainfall chemistry.

Why it happens

Heavy rain increases volume relative to dissolved chemicals, so FC, salt, and CYA readings can all trend down on a percentage basis. Organic debris and runoff add chlorine demand and can cloud water. Acid rain extremes are uncommon but possible—usually the bigger issue is load + dilution.

What to do

  • After a storm: clean skimmer, brush, run filter, then test FC, pH, and alkalinity.
  • Shock if you see high combined chlorine, algae, or heavy organic load.
  • Recalculate doses using updated volume if you had significant overflow or top-off.

Safe ranges / timing

Bring FC back to 1–3 ppm (typical pools) before swimming. Retest 30–60 minutes after chemical adds with pump running.
