How Pool Temperature Affects Chlorine

Hotter water usually burns through free chlorine faster because chemical reactions and microbial growth accelerate—expect higher daily chlorine demand above roughly 85–90°F unless load is very low.

Why it happens

Sanitizer oxidizes contaminants and kills organisms. Warmer temperatures increase reaction rates and can favor algae and biofilm if FC slips. Sun and bather load stack on top—summer is rarely “one variable.”

What to do

  • Test FC more often on heat waves; raise target slightly within safe label ranges if you consistently dip low.
  • Keep circulation strong so you’re not measuring stagnant warm pockets.
  • Pair with summer demand guidance and shock when CC climbs.

Safe ranges / timing

Maintain 1–3 ppm FC for typical pools (hot tubs higher). Re-test 30–60 minutes after adds when adjusting in heat.
