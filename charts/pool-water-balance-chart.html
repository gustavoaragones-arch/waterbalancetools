Pool Water Balance Chart

pH, alkalinity, and chlorine work together. Balance in this order: alkalinity first, then pH, then chlorine.

1. Alkalinity (80–120 ppm)

Buffers pH so it stays stable

↓ 2. pH (7.2–7.6)

Affects chlorine effectiveness and comfort

↓ 3. Chlorine (1–3 ppm)

Sanitizes and keeps water safe

Why this order?

  • Alkalinity acts like a cushion for pH. If alkalinity is wrong, pH will swing when you add acid or base.
  • pH determines how well chlorine works. High pH (above 7.6) weakens chlorine; low pH can irritate skin.
  • Chlorine kills bacteria and algae. Add it last so pH and alkalinity don’t interfere with dosing.
