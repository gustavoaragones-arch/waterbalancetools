When Should You Add Chlorine to a Pool?

Add chlorine when free chlorine falls below your target—often in the evening to reduce UV loss. In most pools, dosing small amounts more often beats rare huge dumps.

Steps

  • Test sanitizer and pH on a steady schedule.
  • Adjust dosing when conditions change (weather, usage, parties).
  • Use the calculator with volume and readings for precise adds.

What This Means

Typical frequency: Check 2–3× weekly; add when below range. Hot tubs may need daily checks. · When it changes: Sun, heat, bather load, and rain all burn sanitizer faster. After shock events, wait until levels are safe before swimming.

Recommended Levels

  • Free chlorine: 1–3 ppm (pools)
  • pH balance: 7.2–7.6
  • Test cadence: 2–3× weekly (busy pools)

Common Questions

Is evening the best time to add chlorine?

Often yes—less immediate UV loss compared to bright midday sun.

Can I add chlorine daily?

Small daily adds can work if tests show need—avoid overdosing.

What if my chlorine reads zero?

Shock or dose per label, then retest—verify volume and product strength.

Does CYA change dosing frequency?

CYA slows chlorine loss to sunlight—your test profile should include it for outdoor pools.

Should I add chlorine before or after swimming?

Follow safe swim ranges—typically dose when below target and wait until readings are safe.
