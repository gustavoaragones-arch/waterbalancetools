What Is Total Alkalinity in a Pool?

Total alkalinity measures the water’s ability to resist pH changes—it acts as a buffer.

What This Means

Think of alkalinity as shock absorption for pH. When alkalinity is in range, pH moves slowly. When it is too low or too high, pH swings or becomes hard to adjust.

Recommended Levels

  • Free chlorine: 1–3 ppm (pools)
  • pH balance: 7.2–7.6
  • Total alkalinity: 80–120 ppm (typical)

Practical impact

Stable alkalinity makes pH and sanitizer easier to manage. Typically test alkalinity weekly in season.

Common Questions

Is alkalinity the same as pH?

No—pH is acidity/basicity; alkalinity is buffering capacity. They are related but different.

What ppm should total alkalinity be?

Many pools use 80–120 ppm; follow your surface and sanitizer system.

Can I raise alkalinity without raising pH much?

Some products increase alkalinity with smaller pH shifts—follow label instructions.

Does high alkalinity lock pH high?

It can make pH harder to lower until alkalinity is adjusted in a controlled way.

How often should I test alkalinity?

Weekly in season is common; test more often after heavy rain or large fills.
